Can we live on the Moon?

   3,148 Ratings | September 14, 2019

The Apollo astronauts are the only people to step foot on the Moon, they only stayed for a few days and had to take
everything they needed to survive. If humans are to live on the Moon, they need to have air to breath, be able to
grow food, make fuel, build shelters and most importantly of all they will need water, H 2 O, and lots of it.

Is there water on the Moon?

The simple answer is Yes and the Indian mission to the Moon, Chandrayaan-1, found it first! But unlike on Earth, where
nearly two-thirds of the surface is covered in liquid water, the Moon is completely dry with no liquid water.

In 2008, the Indian space mission Chandrayaan-1 discovered ice underground in deep craters at the Lunar North Pole. Since then other missions have found ice at the South Pole. It is estimated there are trillions of metric tons of water ice hidden where the Sun does not shine, inside the shadows of crater rims. Plenty to live on, if we can get to it.

Dr. Paul D. Spudis, American lunar scientist, said: "You can drink it, use it to shield from cosmic radiation, use it in food and sanitation, and crack it into oxygen to breathe. In the form of liquid hydrogen and oxygen, it is the most powerful chemical rocket propellant known. Water is the most useful substance in space."

Shelter

Somewhere to call home away from the heat of the day and cold of the night near the ice is essential.

A cave would provide shelter and the Moon Base proposed by NASA is just that, a cave near the Lunar South Pole with a
cover over the entrance.

Fortunately, Japanese scientists have discovered a huge cave, 50 km long, which could be used as a Moon Base, safe from
solar radiation and meteors. Just seal up the entrance.

Does that mean the first people to live on the Moon might be cave men and women?

Well yes, but they will have a lot more than stone tools to live!

What about air?

The air we breathe on Earth is about 80% nitrogen, N2(g), and 20% oxygen, O2(g). Oxygen can be made by electrolysis, the process of passing an electric current through water: 2 H2O(l)  2 H2(g) + O2(g). The N2(g) would need to be found on the Moon. Great news, in 2004 the NASA Genesis Spacecraft detected nitrogen on the Moon’s surface.
